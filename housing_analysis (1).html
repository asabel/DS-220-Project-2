This depth allows analysts to explore how different factors influence housing sale prices and build predictive models, making it a strong foundation for both exploratory data analysis and applied data science.

Handle the missing values. First, fill categorical variables with the value "None". Then fill numerical variables with 0 where data is missing. For LotFrontage, apply a different approach by imputing missing values using the median. Specifically, first fill using the median LotFrontage within each neighborhood, and then use the overall median LotFrontage for any remaining missing values.
In [6]:
# Handle missing values

# Categorical features
cat_fill_none = [
    "Pool QC", "Misc Feature", "Alley", "Fence",
    "Mas Vnr Type", "Fireplace Qu",
    "Garage Type", "Garage Finish", "Garage Qual", "Garage Cond",
    "Bsmt Exposure", "BsmtFin Type 1", "BsmtFin Type 2",
    "Bsmt Qual", "Bsmt Cond",
    "Electrical"
]
for col in cat_fill_none:
    housing[col] = housing[col].fillna("None")


# Numerical features
num_fill_zero = [
    "Mas Vnr Area",
    "Bsmt Full Bath", "Bsmt Half Bath",
    "BsmtFin SF 1", "BsmtFin SF 2", "Bsmt Unf SF",
    "Total Bsmt SF",
    "Garage Area", "Garage Cars", "Garage Yr Blt"
]
for col in num_fill_zero:
    housing[col] = housing[col].fillna(0)

# Lot Frontage  median by neighborhood
housing["Lot Frontage"] = housing["Lot Frontage"].fillna(
    housing.groupby("Neighborhood")["Lot Frontage"].transform("median")
)

# If still missing, fill with overall median
housing["Lot Frontage"] = housing["Lot Frontage"].fillna(housing["Lot Frontage"].median())

# Confirm there are no more missing values
print(housing.isna().sum().sort_values(ascending=False).head(10))

From these graphs, we are able to analyze multiple factors surrounding this data set. These insights may be useful to people interested in buying housing in the area as well as realtors, who can use this information to target clients. This data can also be used by realtor companies to analyze factors such as sale seasons and generate accurate house prices based on house attributes. This data analysis reveals how property value is primarily driven by a combination of overall quality, living space, and functional amenties, which can be summarized in four catagories

1. Quality: The strognest predictors of SalePrice are Overall Quality and Above-Grade Living Area. Homes with high quality material and finish tend to have higher prices. Square footage and price also show a strong positive linear correlation.
2. Amenities: Having garage or basement also generally boosts a house's sale price, as the highest price houses tend to have finished basements and garages with larger capacities. Additional features, such as fireplaces and wood decks are also prominent in the upper 10% of homes.
3. Location: House prices also have a trend across locations, showing how some neighborhoods tend to be more expensive than others.
4. Year Built: Having a home built or remodeled more recently also tends to show a correlation with price.

As all these factors seem to influence the price of a home, we can extrapolate this information as factors that home buyers are most interested in. The data suggests that home buyers are most interested in high quality homes with large square footage. The most common high value homes typically include a high overall quality, at least two garage spaces, and a basement.
